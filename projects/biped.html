Overview. PiB is a boom-constrained, planar bipedal robot built as my funded MSc dissertation project. It's a five-link, four-actuator system with point-foot geometry, designed to walk on a rotating boom while I develop and validate torque-based dynamic control.

The project spans the full stack: mechanical design and gear reduction, embedded motor control over CAN bus, kinematic and dynamic modeling, and — eventually — a head-to-head comparison of classical control against reinforcement learning, evaluated in the same simulator for a fair comparison.

02

Control

Early bench testing confirmed a core finding that shaped the whole project: position-mode control is enough for static tasks, but insufficient for walking. It reacts without a model of the system, can't handle impacts, and is incompatible with the torque outputs that Hybrid Zero Dynamics (HZD) requires. That result set the direction toward a full torque-based control pipeline.

  • Implemented and validated a square-trajectory simulation directly on the physical right leg.
  • Tuned compliant, spring-like joint behaviour by lowering pos_gain into the 3–5 range with proportionally reduced velocity and integrator gains — and resolved the resulting "spinout detected" faults by raising vel_limit and aligning current_soft_max across joints.
  • Built the URDF kinematic model (four joints, parallelogram-linkage mimic constraints) as the foundation for Pinocchio-based inverse dynamics.
03

Current status & next steps

Actively working through the trajectory-to-torque pipeline: finalising URDF inertial parameters (tied to the torso CAD), validating against the manipulator equation at rest (G(q) = τ as a gravity-compensation sanity check), and moving toward full HZD-based gait generation.

  • Assemble the robot to the boom and achieve stable circular walking.
  • Implement reinforcement learning in Isaac Lab as a second controller, with sim-to-sim transfer through Gazebo for a fair comparison against the classical baseline.
  • Publish the dissertation manuscript.
