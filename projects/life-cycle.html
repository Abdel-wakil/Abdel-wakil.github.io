Overview. A quantified life cycle analysis and redesign of a mechanical lifting jack, aimed at reducing its environmental impact without compromising performance. The project combined LCA methodology with structural simulation to make design decisions on real data rather than intuition.

01

Approach

  • Established a baseline carbon footprint of around 560 kg CO₂ for the existing design, primarily driven by material choice and manufacturing processes.
  • Ran a topology optimisation in ANSYS to identify where material could be removed while preserving 55–60% of the starting mass.
  • Re-ran a static structural analysis on the optimised geometry to validate that load-bearing performance was maintained post-redesign.
  • Reduced the total carbon footprint to 440 kg CO₂ — an approximate 22% reduction — through the combined material and process changes.
