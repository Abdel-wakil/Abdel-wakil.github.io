Overview. A self-directed robotic arm project, iteratively developed from a 3-degree-of-freedom prototype to a full 6DOF system. The focus throughout was precision, torque, and efficiency — and doing it on hardware that's cheap to reproduce and reliable on consumer-grade 3D printers.

Mechanical design

  • Evolved drive technology across iterations, from planetary gearboxes to hybrid cycloidal–belt transmissions, trading off precision, torque, and efficiency at each stage.
  • Optimised manufacturability by standardising on off-the-shelf hardware — 608-2z ball bearings, Nema 17 stepper motors, and standard bolts — keeping every part low-cost, easy to print, and reliable to reproduce.
02

Control architecture

The arm runs a two-layer control system, splitting the problem into planning and execution:

  • High-level: ROS + MoveIt, running in a VM, generates joint angles from user-defined end-effector poses set in RViz, solved via inverse kinematics.
  • Low-level: Python control scripts take those joint angles and translate them into real-time stepper motor commands.
